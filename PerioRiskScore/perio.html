💡 AI Recommendations
Personalized home care and clinical recommendations based on your risk profile
🧵
Start Flossing Daily
Use dental floss or interdental brushes daily before brushing. Flossing removes 40% more plaque than brushing alone, especially from the gum line where periodontal disease starts.
⚡ High Priority
🪥
Upgrade Your Brushing Technique
Use a soft-bristled brush at a 45° angle to the gumline. Brush for 2 minutes twice daily using circular motions. Consider an electric toothbrush for better plaque removal.
📌 Medium Priority
💊
Use Antiseptic Mouthwash
Rinse with chlorhexidine or cetylpyridinium chloride mouthwash for 30 seconds after brushing. This reduces bacterial load by up to 60% and targets areas the brush misses.
📌 Medium Priority
😴
Maintain 7-8 Hours Sleep
Sleep deprivation increases systemic inflammation which directly worsens periodontal disease. Your current 7h is good — maintain this consistently for optimal gum health.
✅ Maintaining Well
🦷
Schedule Dental Check-up Immediately
Your assessment shows you haven't visited a dentist recently. Professional cleaning removes calculus (hardened plaque) that brushing cannot remove. Schedule within 2 weeks.
⚡ High Priority
🩺
HbA1c Monitoring
Schedule a blood test to monitor your HbA1c levels. Keep it below 5.7% for optimal periodontal health. Diabetes control is directly linked to gum disease severity.
📌 Medium Priority
📋
Periodontal Screening
Request a full periodontal chart from your dentist measuring pocket depth, bleeding on probing, and bone levels. This should be done every 6 months given your risk profile.
📌 Medium Priority
🥗
Anti-inflammatory Diet
Increase intake of omega-3 fatty acids (fish, flaxseed), antioxidants (berries, leafy greens), and vitamin C (citrus fruits). These reduce systemic inflammation and support gum tissue repair.
📌 Medium Priority
🍬
Reduce Sugar Intake
Sugar feeds oral bacteria and spikes blood glucose (affecting HbA1c). Limit refined sugars to under 25g per day. Choose xylitol-containing gum as it inhibits bacterial growth.
⚡ High Priority
💧
Stay Hydrated
Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily. Saliva is your mouth's natural defense against bacteria. Dehydration reduces saliva flow, increasing bacterial activity and plaque formation.
✅ Low Priority
